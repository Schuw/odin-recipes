Instructions:

  1. Cook the Chicken

    1. In a pot, cook the chicken breasts in water with a pinch of salt until fully cooked (about 15‚Äì20 minutes).
    2. Remove the chicken and shred it using two forks.
    3. Reserve 2 cups of the chicken broth for the dough.

  2. Prepare the filling

    1. In a pan, heat the olive oil and saut√© the chopped onion and garlic until soft.
    2. Add the shredded chicken, salt, pepper, and optional parsley. Mix well.
    3. Turn off the heat and stir in the cream cheese. Set aside to cool.

  3. Make the Dough

    1. In a saucepan, bring the 2 cups of chicken broth and butter to a boil.
    2. Once boiling, add the flour all at once and stir vigorously until it forms a smooth, firm dough and pulls away from the sides of the pan.
    3. Let the dough cool slightly, then knead it on a clean surface until smooth

  4. Shape the Coxinhas

    1. Take a small portion of dough (about the size of a golf ball) and flatten it in your hand.
    2. Place a spoonful of chicken filling in the center.
    3. Fold the dough around the filling and shape it into a teardrop (thicker at the base and pointed at the top).

  5. Bread the Coxinhas

    1. Dip each coxinha into the beaten egg, then roll it in breadcrumbs to coat.

  6. Fry Until Golden

    1. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan over medium-high heat.
    2. Fry the coxinhas in batches until golden brown and crispy (about 3‚Äì4 minutes).
    3. Drain on paper towels.

Tip: Coxinhas can be frozen before frying. Just bread them and freeze on a tray, then transfer to a bag once solid. Fry straight from frozen when ready to eat.
